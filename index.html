About

01 / Background

My work sits at the intersection of gender, natural resources and livelihoods. I coordinate field activities that engage communities directly — designing tools that capture how women, men, youth and persons with disabilities experience the same intervention differently, and translating that into programming that actually reaches them.

That's meant leading research coordination for adolescent mothers rebuilding their lives after early pregnancy in Burkina Faso and Malawi — through skills training, conditional cash transfers for school re-entry and childcare, and livestock-based economic empowerment. It's also meant working with artisanal fishing communities and Beach Management Units along Kenya's coast to understand gendered post-harvest losses, and convening county leadership on the social impact of new energy and livestock infrastructure.

Across every project, the throughline is the same: get close enough to a community to collect data that's actually true, then turn it into something that changes how a programme is designed.
